Savings and Budgeting

Why should you save and budget your money?

There are many reasons why you should save and/or budget your money. You may want a big purchase and saving money would help by splitting that large sum into managable chunks. You can also save for long term goals, such as retirement. Saving and budgeting also helps with (bad) spending habits. One is less likely to not spend as much if they do not have the money to do so. Having a set amount for certain categories allows for control over how much is spent. It is also important to save money in case of an emergency.



How much money should you save?

In case of emergencies, you should have an emergency fund for about 3 to 6 months. This fund should include enough for rent, utilities, food, and other necessities. For other savings goals, you should build your savings and budgeting schedule based on how much you can afford to save. Generally, the 50/30/20 rule is suggested: 50% for essentials such as rent, utilties, and food; 30% for wants(discretionary spending); and 20% for savings.



How do you create a budget?

As suggested previously, the 50/30/20 rule can be used to create a budget: 50% for essentials such as rent, utilties, and food; 30% for wants(discretionary spending); and 20% for savings. Once you figure out your net income, you can adjust these categories accordingly. You should also revisit your budget every few months, or if your financial situation changes dramatically. It is also recommended that you set goals, as this can motivate you to keep track of your spending and not break budget. You can use spreedsheets or budgeting apps to help you keep trakc of your spendings.
