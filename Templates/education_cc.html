Credit Cards

What is a credit card?

A credit card is a card issued by a financial institution and allows the cardholder to borrow funds from that institution on the condition that the money borrowed is paid back in full with any additional charges or interest.



What is the difference between a credit card and a debit card?

A debit card allows the cardholder to withdraw funds they have deposited at the bank while a credit card allows the cardholder to borrow funds from the institution. However, if these funds are not paid by the due date, the cardholder would have to pay an interest fee in addition to the balance due. Credit cards may also offer better consumer protection, however, many newer debit cards are offering better protection. Credit cards may also include perks such as cash back, points, or rewards.



What are the advantages of using a credit card?

Advantages of using a credit card include building a credit history, rewards, convenience, fraud protection, and the option to pay over time.



What is an APR?

This is the annual percentage rate, the interest rate the cardholder will pay on the balance carried from month to month. Some cards charge different rates on different types of balances, including purchases, balance transfers (debts moved to the card from other accounts) and cash advances (cash withdrawn with the card, usually at an ATM). Some cards, though not many, also have penalty APRs, which are imposed after a late payment.
