Investing

What is investing?

In short, Investing is a way to set aside your money while you're busy with life and have that money work for you so that you can fully reap the rewards of your labor in the future. The goal of investing is to put your money to work in one or more types of investment vehicles, such as stocks, in the hopes of growing your money over time. Let's say that you have $1,000 set aside to invest and you're ready to invest, or maybe you only have $10 extra a week, and you'd like to get into investing. Either way, both methods of investing let your money grow itself.

So what can I invest in?

There are many types of stocks that you can invest in. Stocks are usually a single company or a group of companies. The Standard and Poor's 500, or simply the S&P 500, is one of the many stock market index that tracks large companies listed on stock exchanges in the United States. In this case, the S&P 500 tracks 500 large companies listed in the US. It is one of the most commonly followed equity indices. Other big name ETFs include IVV, VTI, VOO, QQQ, VEA, IEFA, AGG, and a lot more

Similarly, You can invest in singular companies such as Apple, Microsoft, Google, Amazon, Facebook, Tesla, etc... You can find the top 50 biggest US companies in the stock market here

How do I invest?

In the modern age of technology, brokers have become digitalized. Brokers are companies that help you buy and sell stock. Brokers such as Fidelity, TD Ameritrade Robinhood, WeBull, are all brokers that function the same in terms of buying and selling stocks for investments.

When you are choosing an online broker you have to think about your needs as an investor. Are you a beginner? Maybe you need a broker that has great educational material about the stock market, or do you only have a small amount of money you can put aside to invest? Some online brokers allow for small minimum deposits which can be a great option for those with limited funds. Or are you always on the go and in need of a broker with a mobile platform? Some online brokers have incredible mobile apps delivering nearly all the features that their desktop counterparts do.
